## Tournament Stories

These are the moments that define why I play. The comebacks, the victories, the losses, and everything I learned along the way.

### May 25th - Minnesota: The Comeback

Competed in Minnesota with Lucas. It was a fun tournament and we had a lot of family support that stayed there for our first 3 matches. It was fun and I'm glad people were there rooting for us.

We also had one of the best comebacks I've ever experienced in a game of volleyball. I'm pretty sure that everyone had counted us out by the time the score was 14-9. I don't really remember too much about what specifically happened except that we had strung together 7 points in a row to come back and win the game 16-14.

That was def a highlight for me. I was float serving but I stayed present and in the moment and they made errors at the right time. I didn't force anything and tried my best each and every point that I was able to.

It felt surreal like I could do no wrong and yet at the same time I didn't really feel overpowered either just a guy in the moment trying to do whatever he could to win.

This is what flow state feels like. Not superhuman. Not invincible. Just present. Just focused. Just doing what needs to be done, point by point.

**The Following Game:**

The following game we played against Soren and Markus. They were a good team but for sure beatable. We weren't able to take advantage of their weaknesses and I think they've been much more battle tested over time.

I don't think I've seen enough people playing high level volleyball as much recently so it was good to watch what is happening in the higher levels of the sport right now.

**Observations:**

I saw that Dylan had a very crisp game plan and he was able to execute at a high level. It seemed like they had the most physicality behind them.

Kevin and his partner were a close second when it comes to gameplan and physicality. They also had the factor of being a tricky lefty which can throw teams off and I think the past two times I've played against lefties I've actually lost to them in a tournament.

Lefties are tricky. The angles are different. The spin is different. You have to adjust your defense and your expectations.

### June 8th - VA Beach with Kris

Played in VA Beach with my boy Kris back. That guy never ceases to amaze me, he is a great athlete, human, and baller.

I think we played pretty well but we weren't making any of the amazing plays that we could have made in order to really make us shine better than our opponents.

**First Match - California Team:**

We lost to a California team that had a pretty solid game plan but I don't think there was anything special about them that should have led to them beating us in a match of beach volleyball. Their serve was good but we weren't able to take advantage of their weaknesses and pressure them.

I think in the first set they blitzed us a bit and I spent too much energy thinking about Dale and also being a nice guy and interfacing with them rather than focusing on my mission to win beach volleyball matches.

This is a key lesson: be nice before and after the match. During the match, focus on winning.

**Middle Matches:**

We sort of had a walk through next two matches that didn't really take much effort, against Rohan where I busted my eye open and Ryan Ludvik who played well but wasn't really in a position to cause us any trouble.

**Final Match - Pat and Mark:**

We then played against Pat and Mark who played extraordinarily well against us, made uncharacteristically few unforced errors and also had some luck thrown in there as well.

It was the type of match that could have been possible to win if it was 3 sets but one game to 28 with all of the weighted issues was incredibly challenging.

We gave them some trouble on the bad side with our jump serves but I think I should have invested more in blocking the hits because there were a few times that they got bailed out by hitting low angles.

### July 27th, 2024 - R. Frost Middle School Victory

Played in a vball tourney at R. Frost Middle school and we won. It was revco 4's grass and the tournament went well.

I didn't play great in pool play but as the day went on I continued to improve and by the time playoffs started I was already into a good groove.

I scored well, played pretty good defense I think, and contributed mentally to a lot of the load.

**MVP Performance:**

Cindy was the mvp with incredible plays at the end of quarters and semis getting blocks in order to finish the matches.

It was a hype and fun day. As prizes we got balls. I also received a pals on pals shirt as part of my award for winning, on account of that I got it all sweaty and dirty throughout the day lol.

**Family Moment:**

It was fun and I'm glad I played. Kaia got to watch some of our games and I think she had a good time watching.

Having family there changes the experience. It's not just about winning anymore - it's about showing them what you love, what you work on, what matters to you.

### August 2nd, 2024 - Qualifier with Kris

Only played one match but it was such a brutal experience. Super hot and blocking full time is tough even if it's for just one match I guess.

Played with Kris back against Marty Lorenz and Justice Anderson.

**The Match:**

Started down in pretty much every set, made some comebacks over time but in the second set we weren't able to pressure them enough to pull out the W.

It feels like Marty was able to dominate a bit and we couldn't really put enough pressure on Justice.

**Score Line:**
- W 21-18
- L 21-19
- L 15-10 (I think)

We were able to steal the first set after being down pretty big which was cool but couldn't capitalize and take the second set, which was a bummer.

**Performance Analysis:**

Kris and I both played pretty well. I think I got blocked a couple of times when I didn't have to. I also made a couple of bad passes and sets.

What we really needed to do was serve more aggro.

This keeps coming up. Serve pressure is the difference maker. When we serve tough, we win. When we don't, we struggle.

### May 12th, 2025 - First Rites, Rehobeth with Brady

Played in First Rites, Rehobeth with Brady on the 10th. It was a pretty fun day overall.

**Pool Play:**

The first 2 games of pool play really weren't all that exciting tbh.

We played a great game against Matt Elias and Ian Capp, who are great grass players but we were able to take advantage of some of their weaknesses when it comes to beach.

**The Turning Point:**

Brady played really well after we made an important decision to switch sides putting him on the left and me on the right. We refocused and our setting and side out game improved tremendously.

Sometimes the smallest adjustments make the biggest difference. Switching sides changed everything.

**Quarter Final:**

Playing our quarter final against David Fish and Ian Porr who were both great guys. (David Fish and I did not get along while he was reffing our semi)

But Brady and I had a pretty close match our first game and then our second game Brady and I played incredibly well basically made zero mistakes and put on a lot of pressure to sort of warstomp their team.

Shots were crisp, we made some great defensive plays and scored almost instantly each time.

**Semi Final:**

Our next match was against Cam and this young kid his name currently escapes me. He's athletic and quite good but slightly raw and still has strides to make as a player.

The first game we won pretty easily with a late push.

The second game we were down big for most of it but had a pretty good comeback from 16-12 to have a 19-18 lead. We hung in there until like 25-23 but I think Brady got aced to put us into a tiebreak.

**The Tiebreak:**

The tiebreak we got an early break and I think we had 6-4 lead, 8-7, then 11-9 and we won 15-10 after I made a few reals in a row.

My serving here was important throughout the match mostly aces made the difference.

**Final:**

Our final match was against Ayden and Carson, the young guns. They play phenomenally, hard to stop. Get their game very simple - passed well and high.

It would take going 100% in order to beat them.

Brady felt like he was cramping but eventually got his head into it and started balling. It was fun playing with him. I like his spirit.

### May 27th, 2025 - Pompano, FL with Angel

Played in a vball tourney with Angel Dache in Pompano FL. Angel picked me up and we drove from Miami, got there really early and had a great breakfast. Had a pretty solid warm up. Angel knew pretty much all of the guys.

He also asked me to play in a couple other tournaments, which I'm inclined to say yes. Idk about the Wilmington one mostly because it's far and I have oncall that weekend.

**The Heat:**

Getting back to the actual event - it was so hot I can't believe it. I was dying on the court. It wasn't really a cardio thing or at least I didn't feel any stitches or lung issues. It may have been that my heart was beating way too fast and I didn't know how to properly slow it down.

**Favorite Quote:**

One of my favorite quotes for the weekend was, "The sun was out and when the sun's that hot there is no where to hide."

It was in response where I said there was probably more I could do to mitigate the impact the heat had on me throughout the tourney.

**First Match:**

The first game was against Ryan Love and Chris Dedo. Angel did a lot on 2 and got a few really important service runs in the third set to secure the W.

**Second Match:**

The second match was against 2 lefties, Ben Vaught who I lost to when playing with Lucas as well. The other person's name was Nate Pugh my first time playing against him.

They both played pretty great and we had difficulty stopping them.

**Physical Reality:**

I really don't think my cardio was up to par again here. In order to compete with these guys I have to spend multiple days a month sprinting until I'm ready to puke. I just don't know how I'm going to get to that level of commitment and effort right now.

I needed to jump serve. I also needed to run up and block and I wasn't able to do either of those.

**What Went Well:**

I felt strong during the tourney and was able to make some amazing plays. I thought I passed and set well too.

My main weaknesses were court vision (shots). I think my block could have been slightly better. I also think my serve was rather lackluster.

### June 30th, 2025 - AVP Big Money: The Comeback

Played in a contender series event and did not qualify again with Angel. I missed many of my shots and fumbled a bit under pressure which is unfortunate.

**However...**

We played in an AVP Big Money afterwards and it started well. We won our first 2 matches, dropped into the loser's bracket after playing the 1 seed, climbed our way back into a semi where we rematched the 1 seed, Skylar Del Sol and Ryan Smith.

**We actually beat them!!**

I had a few blocks and it led us to an incredible comeback.

**The Finals:**

We then played Spencer and JD in the finals. We dropped the first set but came back and won.

I was able to prove myself which is a really powerful and good feeling.

This is what it's all about. Not the qualifying. Not the perfect play. But the grind. The comeback. The belief that you can win even when you're in the loser's bracket. Even when you're facing the 1 seed. Even when you drop the first set in the finals.

You keep fighting. You keep believing. And sometimes, you win.
