Another moment, fresh and blessed,  
I suggest you ingest what we suggest.  
Under our roof is a baby girl—  
pink fists curled, lungs testing the air.  

Are times changing?  
Or are we still living in a world  
that keeps on misbehaving  
while the withering ones won't stop complaining?

Growth is eternal but the sails won't stay hoisted without a ship.  
A ship won't be built unless it has soil—  
roots before masts, dirt under fingernails.  

Plastic hearts can't make it, how can they grow?  
A robotic heart can't fake it, never hurts to know.

No truth, absolute proof is aloof.  
Support your rapport lest you ignore  
all chances of composing what you're here for.  

Closer to where I'm supposed to be,  
but forcing tragedies keep getting the best of me.

Crowds doomed to battlefields with sparked yields,  
but soon I'll succumb to sleeping.  
Down in the slums dreams are thicker,  
the sharks catch you quicker,  
and minds never stop racing.

At the mountain top, air's thin,  
all your masks are trapped within.  
Breathing doesn't make you a master  
when surrendering couldn't come faster.  

I don't think I'll ever surpass her—  
my ladder is currently shambled plaster,  
luckily unentitled dogs aren't intended  
to prevent the natural disaster.
