Some days flashing through the past,  
I've been looking away—  
sort of choked up, dazed,  
it's hard to stay through this winter play.

Can you hear me, soft embrace?  
A suddenly airtight creeping frays my might,  
frost on the windowpane, breath held too long.  
Whisper gently, dearly to me,  
can you solve this plight?

I'm sorry lover, my voice got lost in what has happened.  
It's stuck somewhere in the past  
and while it's soothing we have no cure—  
no salve for words that won't come forward.  
Stewards strike through, and they bring me toward you.  
My haven was born on uncertainty,  
built from this lonely memory,  
foundation of maybe and almost.

We always tend to decide chapters close on the ends we send,  
but they seem to pass us by and we never notice.  
Farewells that choked us up don't seem like endings—  
not the long nights spent thinking of our motives,  
not the gasping in the dark.  
Until our final slumber, I beckon,  
let's pretend our rules will bend.

I've heard you melt, I've seen the damage one has dealt.  
My blows may have grazed you  
but the world will never be the same—  
small cracks in the foundation, water seeping through.  
Can my sadness make a difference  
or is there no one to blame?  
Why can't we all live simply?  
There's something burning an ego's flame,  
and we're all holding the match.
