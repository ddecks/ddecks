## Training and Practice

The tournaments are where you prove yourself. But practice is where you build yourself.

### June 12th, 2024 - Structured Training

Practiced with Rohan, James, and Scott. I started off the session working on some setting stuff with Rohan, then we did some burpee downball warmup.

**Drill Progression:**

We then did a passing to setting drill that we eventually modified to include hitting. We then worked on some box drills for hard driven and shot run down transition opportunities. We also worked on some clutch game like situations.

I feel as if there was some other things in between those drills that I'm now forgetting but that's fine.

**Key Takeaway:**

I think from this experience I'm continuing to learn how to facilitate good environments for learning and training.

This is important. It's not just about getting reps. It's about creating an environment where everyone is learning, everyone is improving, everyone is engaged.

### June 19th, 2024 - Meeting Leorre

Met a new person playing today from San Diego, his name will be updated later - Leorre - but he was a pretty good player.

**Session Structure:**

We started at 6am, took a pretty chill warm up:
1. Setter warm up with Rohan
2. Light pepper
3. No jump
4. Hitting
5. Played games

**First Game - Learning Experience:**

Lost my first game with Leorre. I experimented with asking him to set me lower than usual. It didn't go great. I also ran up to block, we experienced issues.

Mostly I think they were from some simple mistakes and the other team were able to side out fairly well.

**Finding the Rhythm:**

Won my next 2 with James and Rohan pretty easily. It mostly stemmed from good defense with James and a really solid side out %.

With Rohan we were able to make a good late game push to pull away and take control of the game.

**Adjustments with Leorre:**

Then played 2 more with Leorre that went much better. He played really well. I think the major deciding factor in the middle of the second game with him was his decision to start blocking for a few points.

They had been pretty effective on the two ball attack option and they weren't putting much pace on it. From there we went on a 3 point string and were able to win the game being up on the 18-17 switch instead of down.

**Key Learning:**

Sometimes the smallest tactical adjustment - like starting to block on a specific play - can swing the entire momentum of a game.

3rd game seemed like a really good side game for us. Steady defense and utilizing the other team's tendencies to our own strength.

### Training Philosophy

Looking back at all these sessions, a few patterns emerge:

**Consistency Matters:**

The sessions where I showed up consistently, even when tired, even when the level wasn't great, even when conditions were bad - those are the sessions that built my game.

**Structured Practice:**

The best sessions had structure. Warm up, drills, games. Not just random playing.

**Partner Chemistry:**

Some partners bring out the best in you. Some don't. It's not always about skill - it's about fit, energy, communication.

**Film Review:**

I need to do more of this. You can't improve what you can't see.

**Physical Preparation:**

Cardio, strength, flexibility - they all matter. You can't just play volleyball and expect to compete at a high level.

**Mental Game:**

Energy matters. Attitude matters. How you approach each point matters.

### Moving Forward

The work never stops. But that's what makes it fun.
