Quick sum: childhood this is one of the absolute goated series for me and one of the formative books that I read during my life. Rand, Matt, Perrin, Elayne, Egwene, Faile, Avienda, Nynaveve, the dark one, mordret, Lanfear, Moriane, The different countries were so well fleshed out and visceral while I was reading it. The book has some Christian undertones. It also gets some major inspiration from other spiritual thoughts, like the pattern that's constantly being woven, the One power, a duality of male and female existence, the snake that's eating itself. The Yin and the Yang essences and reincarnation that dominate Eastern belief. The whole premise of the story is set around a battle between the world and the dark one. I missed some of the symbolism at the end but Rand is the main character a reincarnated version of a previous chosen one who basically self-destructed the world causing a huge cataclysm last time the dark one was trying to raise to prominence.
