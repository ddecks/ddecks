Quick sum: This book has been slightly mindtrippy lately, it also requires a lot of attention. I really enjoy the authors annotating methods.  This book has been fairly crazy lately my favorite aspects are the borders between different regions in space. An imagination of interactions between sentient societies.  The descriptions of the alien species in this book are so interesting. I love how the author doesn't curtail to the human species but at the same time extenuates some of their best qualities. He also captures incredibly advanced in a way that explains motive without compromising on any of the incredulity that a human would experience trying to unravel the mystery. I do take an issue with how the most powerful character in the story was handled, the blight, it's possible that I was sleeping so I didn't fully understand what happened but it seemed like one of those quest like novels where attaining the chalice or object of the quest just resulted in everything magically being recovered and the author leaves all the hard parts that the fallout of the universe would result in to the reader's imagination.
