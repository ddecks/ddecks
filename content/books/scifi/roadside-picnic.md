It was pretty quick of a read, I found myself enjoying the state of the MCs mind during the ending. A true human expression of soul that's searching for answers but ultimately finding nothing. I'd agree with your points, something I enjoy about most Russian books is the establishment usually feels like it's own fully formed character. I can usually feel expectations of society on a character in a realistic way. This continued to be the case for this book as well. The foreword made me want to go on a Le Guin binge not for the first time.
