Quick sum: This book is to help distill the habits in your life. Assess them for what they are openly and honestly with yourself. self-help, build a habit build your life
compounding effect - 
Pick something that fits in with your natural gifts, when you are good at something you will generally want to keep at it.
Environment plays a critical role in defining habit behaviors, the most significant contributor is visual environment. Possible to build new environments in order to help curb old habits. In order to break a habit break exposure to the cue that causes the habit in the first place. 
One of the best strategies to build better habits is to join a culture where the desired behavior is the normal behavior. Make bad habits seem unattractive. 
When getting good at something and it becomes a habit, expect you to get bored with the thing, when you are bored expect that you may lose incentive to maintain the habit. Also expect your routine to dominate and there will be gaps in your assessment of peak performance. Being deliberate in your action and approach to practicing will result in your performance improvements. There are decisive moments that deliver tremendous portions of impact each day. Making the right decisions in those moments will lead to compounding better decisions for the rest of the day. 
Good habits should not feel like a challenge to perform, the whole task itself may be difficult but the first two minute queue of the task should be easy. I never felt regretful after doing something that was good for me. I never felt bad after the run was over, after the workout was done. 
Make it satisfying and immediately responsive when performing good habits to an acknowledged reward. 
Track habits in order to measure progress. Add instant cost to bad habits.
