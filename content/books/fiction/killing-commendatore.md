Quick sum: I enjoyed the book, it was a good story. Fun fact mura means uneven and kami means god so Murakami can be portrayed as the uneven god. Lol I looked up the meaning in japanese and it was above the village so I'll just combine the two the uneven god who lives above the village. I've read many of his books. 
This one was about an artist, he had a sister who died when he was young. As an artist he started to paint portraits in order to make money. He eventually found a girl who he liked enough to marry but their relationship wasn't happy. His wife wouldn't have any kids with him and eventually ended their marriage, citing that she had been cheating on him for ~8months. He went on a journey around Japan in his beat up car, the story truly begins when he moves into a famous artist's house who is the father of a friend from school. It's a rural place and he starts teaching art classes. Eventually he is approached by a wealthy dynamic individual who asks him to paint his portrait, The MC hears a bell at night and discovers a shrine that links to the spirit world. He finds a character in a hidden painting has come to life. The tycoon has a secret, he has a daughter that has never met him and she lives on the same mountain. The tycoon asks if he could paint his daughter as well. They agree but eventually the tycoon falls in love with his daughter's caretaker. The daughter doesn't know who the tycoon is and grows suspicious of him going into his property and investigating him she ends up trapped, stumbling on her mothers clothes in the process. The MC kills the commendatore the painting character come to life also a character in Mozart's [Don Giovanni opera](https://www.google.com/url?sa=t&source=web&rct=j&opi=89978449&url=https://www.youtube.com/watch%3Fv%3DIoc9shJa_lI&ved=2ahUKEwiKk8Gw3NGIAxU2ElkFHdWRO9UQwqsBegQICRAG&usg=AOvVaw0SrsT64mFrjhv65hRWBZmG) , and follows the hidden character into a secret plane where he learns that his ex-wife was pregnant but that he could somehow be the father in a cosmic way. He eventually reunites with his ex-wife and they become a couple again. 
I thought the book had some interesting ethereal qualities. It wasn't my favorite hence the slightly lower rating but it definitely captivated me. I'm wondering what I'll take away from it years from now. It followed with Murakami's usual way of describing sexual scenarios that are so realistic to me and yet not cringy like I typically experience during those situations.
